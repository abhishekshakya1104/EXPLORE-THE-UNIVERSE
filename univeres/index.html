üå† Andromeda Galaxy Our Galactic Neighbor

Future Collision with Milky Way

The Andromeda Galaxy (M31) is our closest spiral galaxy neighbor, located about 2.5 million light-years away. It's larger than the Milky Way with about 1 trillion stars.

In about 4.5 billion years, the Milky Way and Andromeda will begin to collide and eventually merge to form a single elliptical galaxy nicknamed "Milkomeda."

üï≥ Black Holes Cosmic Mysteries

Gravity's Ultimate Victory

Event Horizon: The point of no return where gravity is so strong that not even light can escape.

Time Dilation: Time runs slower near a black hole due to extreme gravity. An hour near a black hole could be years elsewhere.

Spaghettification: The stretching effect on objects falling into a black hole due to tidal forces.

The first image of a black hole (M87*) was captured in 2019 by the Event Horizon Telescope.

‚òÑ Meteoroids, Asteroids, and Comets Solar System Debris

Meteoroids

Small rocky or metallic bodies in space. When they enter Earth's atmosphere, they become meteors (shooting stars). If they survive and hit the ground, they're called meteorites.

Asteroids

Larger rocky bodies, mostly found in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter. Ceres is the largest at 940 km diameter. Some are potentially hazardous to Earth.

Comets

"Dirty snowballs" of ice, rock, and dust. When near the Sun, they develop spectacular tails. The Oort Cloud, far beyond Pluto, is home to trillions of comets.

üåô Moon and Artificial Satellites Earth's Companions

Earth's Moon

  • Diameter: 3,474 km (about 1/4 Earth's size)
  • Average distance: 384,400 km
  • Lunar day: 29.5 Earth days (same as its orbit period)
  • Formed from debris after a Mars-sized body collided with early Earth
  • Only celestial body beyond Earth visited by humans (Apollo missions)

Artificial Satellites

Communication

Enable TV, radio, internet, and phone signals across the globe. Geostationary satellites remain fixed above one point on Earth.

Navigation

GPS satellites allow precise location tracking. The system requires at least 24 satellites for global coverage.

Earth Observation

Monitor weather, climate, agriculture, and natural disasters. Some can resolve objects less than a meter across.
